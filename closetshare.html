App Overview And Video Showcase

ClosetShare is an app that aims to inspire individuals to enhance their fashion sense and discover their unique style. It provides a platform for fashion enthusiasts to connect, share, and engage with local and global fashion communities.

Users can showcase their outfits by uploading photos and sharing detailed information about each piece, including where they were purchased, making it easier for others to find and buy similar items. Also, the app allows users to explore a wide range of outfit inspirations, fostering creativity and collaboration.

ClosetShare’s goal is to bridge the gap between personal style and community-driven fashion, encouraging self-expression and shared inspiration.

App Concept Ideation

App Target Audience

People who are unfamiliar with fashion or how to look good, and need inspirations from other people. People who wants to share their own outfits. Finally, people who dont know where to buy certain outfit pieces.

As I was seeking appropriate app ideas, I looked to one common question many ask every morning: “What do I wear today?”

Using platforms such as Pinterest as inspirations, I came to the app idea of a platform that not only specializes in providing outfit ideas for its users, but provide tools to help users replicate these outfit ideas.

To theatrically ensure a consistent flow of outfit ideas, I also proposed outfit sharing to be another primary feature, backed up by a ranking system as further incentive for engagement.

Design Process and Iterations

01

Initial App UI/UX Sketches

With a finalized app concept and a list of featurest, I created UI sketches for core user activities. This includes the home page, where outfit browsing will take place, as well as detailed outfit views and outfit creation.

02

App Stylings

For the app’s font and color choices, my partner and I aimed to create an elegant and luxurious atmosphere, but still welcoming enough for the general audience.

For these purposes, I proposed using a vibrant purple, a color that often represents luxury and royalty, as the app’s highlight color. The rest of the app visuals were kept neutral and subtle to help push the outfit pictures as primary focuses.

For typography, I selected Poppins for titles and Nunito for body text and captions. They conveys a sense of elegance and clarity, while still feeling friendly and minimalistic.

Key Reflections

Adaption is Key

Development plans will likely need to be adjusted as issues emerge. Therefore, being able to adapt the plan and development process to navigate around and resolve these issues is an important skill to have.

An example emerged during development was when I needed to allocated time during development to research and implement other external storage means as firebase had proven ineffective in image storage, which I managed to pull through by integrating Imgur API.

Design choice vs implementation

Due to the project having a strict time limit, there were occasions where I had to opt for a simpler approach for design implementations, as any design that I’ve made will also need to be implemented through code.

Such as the waterfall like card layout that naturally guides the user’s eyes downwards, where a simpler parallel layout was used for actual implementation instead.

Development feasibility is something that should also be taken into account during initial ideation steps, especially when constraints are in play.
