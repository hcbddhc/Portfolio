App Overview And Video Showcase

ClosetShare is an app that aims to inspire individuals to enhance their fashion sense and discover their unique style. It provides a platform for fashion enthusiasts to connect, share, and engage with local and global fashion communities.

Users can showcase their outfits by uploading photos and sharing detailed information about each piece, including where they were purchased, making it easier for others to find and buy similar items. Also, the app allows users to explore a wide range of outfit inspirations, fostering creativity and collaboration.

ClosetShare’s goal is to bridge the gap between personal style and community-driven fashion, encouraging self-expression and shared inspiration.

App Concept Ideation

App Target Audience

People who don’t know what fashion is or how to look good, need inspirations from other. People who wants to share their fits. people who dont know where to buy certain pieces.

As I was seeking appropriate app ideas, I looked to one common question many ask every morning: “What do I wear today?”

Using platforms such as Pinterest as inspirations, I came to the app idea of a platform that not only specializes in providing outfit ideas for its users, but provide tools to help users replicate these outfit ideas.

To theatrically ensure a consistent flow of outfit ideas, I also proposed outfit sharing to be another primary feature, backed up by a ranking system as further incentive for engagement.

Design Process and Iterations

01

Initial App UI/UX Sketches

With a finalized app concept, and a list of features that the app should implement, I created sketches of how the UI will look like for core user activities. This includes the home page, where outfit browsing will take place, as well as detailed outfit views and outfit creation.

02

App Stylings

For the app’s font and color choices, my partner and I aimed to create an elegant and luxurious atmosphere, but still welcoming enough for the general audience.

For these purposes, I proposed using a vibrant purple, a color that often represents luxury and royalty, as the app’s highlight color. At the same time, keep the rest of the app visuals neutral and subtle to help push the outfit pictures as primary focuses.

For typography, I selected Poppins for titles and Nunito for body text and captions. The fonts help conveys a sense of elegance and clarity, while still feeling friendly and minimalistic, complementing the visual identity that we had envisioned.

Key Reflections

Adaption is Key

While it is important to lay out development plans as well as set deadline and goals early on, these plans will likely need to be adjusted as issues emerge. Therefore, being able to adapt the plan and development process to navigate around and resolve these issues is an important skill to have.

One major issue that arise was our initial plan to only utilize Firebase for outfit data storage. Later in the development process, we found that image data is too big for the limited amount of external storage we have access to via Firebase. So we had to repivot and seek alternative means of image storage instead. Consequently, I allocated time during development for research of other external storage means, and Imgur API’s integration after I’ve chosen it for its unlimited storage space.

Design choice vs implementation

As the project encompasses both design and development, any design that I’ve made, I’ll need to make it a reality through code. Due to the project having a strict time limit, there were occasions where I had to opt for a simpler approach for design implementations.

Such as the outfit card displays while browsing outfits. While the finalized mockup design illustrated an asymmetrical, waterfall like card layout that naturally guides the user’s eyes downwards, implementing it in React Native have proven troublesome. As a result, a simpler parallel layout was used for the final prototype.

One key takeaway is that development feasibility is something that should also be taken into account during initial ideation steps, especially when constraints are in play. If we had more time however, we would definitely prioritize implementing all finalized design choices, as well as tackle some current visual consistency issues.
