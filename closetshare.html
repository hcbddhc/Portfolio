App Overview And Video Showcase

ClosetShare is an app that aims to inspire individuals to enhance their fashion sense and discover their unique style, encouraging self-expression and shared inspiration. It provides a platform for fashion enthusiasts to connect, share, and engage with local and global fashion communities.

Users can explore a wide range of outfit inspirations uploaded by other users, including photos and shared detailed information about each outfit piece, such as where they were purchased, making it easier for others to find and buy similar items.

The Problem and Opportunity

Primary Research Question

How might we know more about the deficiencies of current avenues available for people looking to find outfit inspirations or to improve their own style?

Many men and women across demographics are looking to dress better, highlighting a strong demand for fashion inspirations. On Reddit, fashion advice communities such as r/malefashionadvice has over 6.3 million followers, and r/femalefashionadvice has over 5.8 million, each with more than 500k weekly visitors.

To understand how people currently learn about fashion, including their pain points and market gaps, I analyzed current online avenues across Reddit, YouTube, and Discord.

Current Avenues and Their Core Issues

AVENUE A

Social Media Influencers

Hard to retrieve outfit idea for later

Tailored towards those with culturally ideal body types

Outside influence via sponsorships

AVENUE B

Pininterest

Cluttered algorithm with ads and AI content

Often lack of information about specific pieces

Lack of search and filter functionalities

AVENUE C

Community Forums (Reddit, Discord)

Mostly questions and people asking for help

In-community bias for outfit posts

Key Takeaway

Fashion lovers need a platform that not only showcases real outfits from real everyday people, but also accommodates diverse body types, offers precise search and filter tools, and helps users find the exact pieces they need to recreate those looks.

Design Process and Iterations

01

Initial App Architecture Template

Using platforms such as Pinterest as inspirations, I came to the app idea of a platform that not only specializes in providing outfit ideas for its users, but provide tools to help users replicate these outfit ideas. To theatrically ensure a consistent flow of outfit ideas, I also proposed outfit sharing to be another primary feature, backed up by a ranking system as further incentive for engagement.

02

Initial App UI/UX Sketches

With a finalized app concept and a list of features, I created UI sketches for core user activities. This includes the home page, where outfit browsing will take place, as well as detailed outfit views and outfit creation.

03

App Stylings

For the app’s font and color choices, my partner and I aimed to create an elegant and luxurious atmosphere, but still welcoming enough for the general audience.

For these purposes, I proposed using a vibrant purple, a color that often represents luxury and royalty, as the app’s highlight color. The rest of the app visuals were kept neutral and subtle to help push the outfit pictures as primary focuses.

For typography, I selected Poppins for titles and Nunito for body text and captions. They conveys a sense of elegance and clarity, while still feeling friendly and minimalistic.

App Mockup Iterations

Key Reflections

Adaption is Key

Development plans will likely need to be adjusted as issues emerge. Therefore, being able to adapt the plan and development process to navigate around and resolve these issues is an important skill to have.

An example emerged during development was when I needed to allocated time during development to research and implement other external storage means as firebase had proven ineffective in image storage, which I managed to pull through by integrating Imgur API.

Design choice vs implementation

Due to the project having a strict time limit, there were occasions where I had to opt for a simpler approach for design implementations, as any design that I’ve made will also need to be implemented through code.

Such as the waterfall like card layout that naturally guides the user’s eyes downwards, where a simpler parallel layout was used for actual implementation instead.

Development feasibility is something that should also be taken into account during initial ideation steps, especially when constraints are in play.
