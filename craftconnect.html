Final Prototype Overview

Topic Context

Secondary research have supported that Vancouver based small independent artisans in the handmade craft business faces problems such as currently earning below-average revenue and having a volatile source of income. Consequently, it is difficult to transform a small handmade craft business into a successful, livable career.

CraftConnect is a proposed web platform that allows handcraft consumers to learn about, connect, and support their local artisans through various interactive features such as a map overview of nearby artisan shops, and an interactive booth page with customized notes sent to artisans, news boards, and more. It also serves as a centralized source of information about local artisans and the avenues they participate in.

The platform was created after four months of research, interviews, and continuous iterations with a team of four. With the initial objective to resolve the high difficulty for handcraft artisans to transform their craft into a successful business, we later directed our design focus to be establishing an effective process for facilitating customer awareness of local artisans, and fostering meaningful connections. Consequently, CraftConnect is our solution to this design focus.

Problem Framing and Field Research

Initial Problem Framing

How might we learn the methods by which artisans in the craft business build up their presence for local in-person avenues (local shops, events), and whether these methods are effective or not?

During initial research, I pioneered the primary research process helped with planning for this phase of the project. I mapped out a number of artisan avenues and assigned each team member to investigate along with a pre-set schedule.

Later on, I contacted over 30 local artisans and major handcraft consumers, and was able to arrange 5 interviews. I also served as the main interviewer for most of the interview process.

The conducted research revealed the importance of finding a niche dedicated target base for financial stability, yet currently artisans have limited opportunities to form meaningful connections with their customers.

Existing measures used in artisan shops tend to be ignored by customers, while markets and individual booths suffer from lower traffic due to the over reliance on word of mouth advertising.

Based on these research, we've also brainstormed possible points of Intervention using journey maps, from both artisan and customer perspectives, and in both shop and market settings.

Concept Iterations

With my business background in mind, I first proposed a post-purchase appreciation and sticker collection program that aims to improve upon existing shop’s lackluster measures to ensure customers remember the artisan they purchase from.

However, later feedback and analysis concluded that the idea was limited in scope and mainly benefited artisans already selling in craft shops, while also posing risk of excessive waste and logistical challenges. Henceforth, I proposed that we digitalize the concept, and include elements from other proposed ideas, such as the got Craft Interactive Initiative, in this concept.

Feature Breakdown

For CraftConnect's design, I mainly contributed to designing the home page map view, along with the associated popup tabs and notification interactions.

I also proposed a number of interactions that the platform can offer, setting the foundation of CraftConnect's booth page and its various features. Moreover, I assisted with the booth page’s interaction design as well.

01

Map Navigation and Filters

The Home page consists of the map view of all the artisans and avenues close to the user’s location, the information is split into 4 categories. (Markets, Shops, Artisans, and the user’s Subscriptions)

Users can switch between each of the 4 filter categories, allowing them to discover new artisans individually or through avenues, and find out where artisans they love are selling.

02

Search Function

I’ve also integrated a search feature in the map view that opens up a full list of artisans and avenues, allowing users to browse through the information in a list format and search using keywords.

03

Notifications and Calender

Notifications can be found on the upper right side through the bell icon. Users can retrieve the most up to date information about the artisans they are following.

A calendar feature is also available for users to keep track of event times for subscribed artisans.

04

Booth Interactions

Upon clicking on the individual artisan icons, users will be directed to a booth page for that artisan. Here users can participate in a variety of interactions that is designed to facilitate connection between artisans and their costumers.

The interactable objects include:

  • Profile
  • Calender
  • Product Shelf
  • Newsboard
  • Tip Jar
  • Notes Box

CraftConnect have received positive feedback from both artisan and craft consumer interviewees, validating the platform’s ability to resolve the original proposed problem.

Key Reflections

Importance of seeking additional perspectives

This project’s long time duration allows for an opportunity to perform extensive research on our initially selected subject matter, and an opportunity to diverge, converge, or repivot concept and solutions. It is through these opportunities that I truly understood the significance of always seeking external and diverse perspectives and opinions.

It wasn't until I’ve actually visited artisan avenues, observed how things are currently being done, and talked to various stakeholders, that possible points of intervention begin to emerge. None of the proposed solutions would had came to fruition without the insights gained from seeking these perspectives through field research.

If we were to have more time, I would definitely advocate for additional user testing to identity further insights, potential improvements, and the effectiveness of the finalized direction. As design as this project have proven, is a continuous iterative process.
