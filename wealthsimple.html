Design Solution Overview

The Problem Space

Investing is an activity everyone should participate in to better their long term financial wellness. The benefits provided by investing is especially valuable among youth, as getting a headstart early would yield the best results long term.

I, along with two peers, seek to explore methods of design that can encourage young adults to invest early in their lifetime to maximize long term financial stability.

With contribution from my peers, I finalized a research questionnaire targeting those aged 19 to 29 that inquired participants regarding their knowledge and opinion of investment as an activity, as well as their confidence level.

Based on insights gathered from the questionnaire’s 21 responses, we formulated the following design problem statement:

Design Problem Statement

There currently lacks an accessible and centralized source of information for young people to get started on long term investments, as the frequently fragmented learning sources raises the barrier of entry.

Wealthsimple: Our Design Platform

To best tackle this problem, I advocated for exploring existing investment related platforms as points of intervention over designing a new one, and finalized Wealthsimple as that platform.

Wealthsimple is a Canadian platform that provides investment services to the public, with the aim to help everyone achieve financial freedom. It was a popular platform referenced within our questionnaire responses. Online consensus gathered from initial research also indicates Wealthsimple as a popular starting point for investing among youth.

* The redesign proposal was created for academic purposes, and the team is not officially affiliated with Wealthsimple.

Research and Wealthsimple Evaluation

Primary Design Question

How might we transform the Wealthsimple app into an intuitive experience that can help reduce the learning difficulties during the investing process, as well as support young and novice investors in building and retaining foundational information relating to investing?

The team navigated the Wealthsimple app to collect insights and perform UX evaluations, while performed HCI research for possible solutions. My research domain for this phase of the project comprised of visual analytics, distributed cognition frameworks, mental models, and information foraging theory.

As an experienced Wealthsimple user, I was also able to provide additional insights based on my personal experience and investment related knowledge while other team members contributed to documenting perspectives from a new user's point of view.

01

High Cognitive Demand

Research from other team members have concluded that processing new information leads to a cognitive load on the learner which can contributes towards poorer decision-making specifically with risk analysis, and increased difficulty to absorb new information.

Wealthsimple’s interface overwhelms beginner users with plentiful of unknown numbers, terms, and acronyms which strains working memory.

The interface have also done little in terms of cues that can help direct users or help with understanding.

02

Unfamiliarity of Mental Models

Following up on my research of mental model theories, investing as a concept appears to lack concrete connection with any existing physical phenomenon, as it is a digital concept. This combined with a general unfamiliarity of investing among youth makes existing mental models, such as portfolio as personal assets and interactive graphs, abstract and disconnecting for young, new investors. 

03

Individual Based Cognitive Process

Support from external sources are hard to come by for newer investors. The process of investing is quite individual oriented with social collaboration being largely absent due to its private nature. Additionally, fragmented sources of information complicates decision making, as investors need to evaluate a large number of external sources such as online forums, videos, financial news.

04

Confusing Visual Representations

Current visual representations used on Wealthsimple, such as the graph used to represent portfolio performance, are not effective instruments for helping users to process information and make decisions.

Not only are they few and far between, existing visual representations are lacking in visual support and forms of interaction.

Finalized Redesign Proposals

With the research insights and intervention goals, we proposed a number of redesigns and new features that can help tackle the problem space. For myself, I’ve proposed the 4 following changes and designed mockups for them.

01

Cues for Improved Understanding

Addition of cues in a number of forms across the entire interface that communicates more information that would support them in navigating through the investing process and in making decisions.

02

Improved Interactable Portfolio Holdings

I advocated for transforming the portfolio holdings screen from a simple list into a visualized and interactable pie chart, a more accurate and recognizable form of representation that organized information into clear categories. Users can also choose between different methods of categorization or switch back to the classic list view via a toggle.

03

Opt In Portfolio Sharing Feature

To allow collaboration between different users to offload the mental load, I proposed an optional community based feature that allows users to share their own portfolios and receive collaborative feedback through likes, Q&As, and other user comments. Users can also view other shared portfolios and ask questions as an information source.

04

Revised Portfolio Performance Chart

I redesigned Wealthsimple’s performance chart to communicate more information through visuals in a digestible manner. I’ve added more relevant data visualization and supporting elements to help with understanding.

Key Reflections

Design solutions should be based on evidence

For my design proposals that aims to ease investment learning curve and provide additional tools to support users during the investment process, all solutions are evidence based, and results of extensive research of peer reviewed research. When design is based on well founded research and concrete evidence, and not simply subjective assumptions, they become far more justifiable.

Importance of “unflattening” in design

Through this project, I experienced first hand the value in the idea of “unflattening”, taking information and knowledge from a diverse set of fields, and combining them to for design solutions. During our research, we explored human cognition theories, social dynamics, economics, and more. Even if these fields and surrounding research seem distant from interface design at a first glance, the evidence collected from these fields still plays an important role in shaping effective solutions.
