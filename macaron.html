Website Idea and My role

For this two person project, while my partner concentrated on crafting a strong and distinctive brand identity through the creation of the logo, supporting visuals, and selecting cohesive colors and fonts, my role focused more so on web design and development, as well as create an user experience that aligns seamlessly with said brand's identity.

The Brand: Macaron

A small Montreal-based patisserie, well known for making light, airy macarons and their cute mascot (a mouse named Ronnie)

The company targets customers of all ages, but especially those with an affinity for cute things and good desserts, individuals who post their food on social media with carefully crafted captions.

Macaron entices potential consumers by offering a seamless ordering experience, with consistent visual design to promote the brand’s reliability - if the website looks so good, imagine how good the macarons taste...

01

Highlighting the Product

With the website's primary purpose to showcase macarons and to serve as a platform for selling these products. I focused primarily on highlighting product images throughout the entire website, while allocating other elements as subtle backdrops.

However, I still aimed to find ways to better integrate the brand’s hand-drawn cartoonish design as the site’s complementary visual language, ensuring a cohesive look that enhances the brand’s unique identity.

02

An Efficient Shopping Experience for Users

The primary function of the website: the online shopping aspect, is one of my major focus while designing the website. My philosophy during development is that, not only should this process be visually aesthetically pleasing to maintain the brand’s playful tone, this process should first and foremost be an seamless experience for users seeking to purchase macarons on the website.

To reinforce the image heavy, minimalistic nature for consistency, I’ve taken into account the number of clicks or scrollable content during the process, the amount of visually distractions on relevant pages, as well as the impression and emotion a prospective customer would experience.

03

Organized Code System for Better Collaboration

As the primary web developer, I took the lead in structuring the codebase to enhance collaboration with my partner as new features and pages were added.

I prioritized creating a readable and well-organized foundation, iteratively refining HTML and CSS elements throughout development to minimize technical debt.

The resulting code system allows for more time to be dedicated to proper visual hierarchy, optimizing page layouts, and refining aesthetic details, helping me in creating a seamless user experience while using the company’s website.

01

Landing Video Showcasing the Product

Following my research of various sites, I’ve created a video showcasing macaron products and our brand identity. This video is displayed in the landing section of the website, creating a strong first impression of the site.

02

Efficient Shopping Process

Clear list of all the products with two types of categorization, with option to view each product in extreme detail. Minimizing the number of clicks needed to select products for an efficient shopping process for users.

03

Home Page Brand Introduction

On top of the landing video, images of products, as well as hand drawn aesthetics elements are integrated within the rest of the home page, further creating a strong brand identity while communicating vital information to users.

Key Reflections

Collaborative handoff from design to development

For this project, my focus was to take into account an existing brand and aesthetics, and figure out a way to best make it shine, as well as focusing on the user experience as they navigate through the site. I've found constant communcation with the brand designer to be the main key of success.

Moreover, as the project involves more than just design, and involves code implementation, it highlighted the importance of clear communication between team members, especially through proper code organization and clear comments. Maintaining a structured and well documented codebase was essential to ensure smooth development and effective collaboration.
