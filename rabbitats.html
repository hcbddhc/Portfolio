Design Strategy and Research

Taking the 4 weeks project duration constraint into account, I was one of the parities arguing against tackling every single aspect of the website during the initial brainstorming process, as it is to my firm belief that it is more ideal to target specific pain points and weaknesses and make appropriate, realistic solutions that would best fit the organization’s unique goals.

As the leading researcher during beginning steps, I’ve done extensive research on not just the organization itself to understand its missions and activities, but the greater context as well, being the continuous population growth of local rabbit colonies. Being the only member that’s located in Richmond at the time also provided me additional reference point to draw from.

From not just my personal experiences, but as well as local news report and social forum insights, one common theme in relation to pet rabbit abandonments, is the lack of education among current and prospective rabbit keepers.

With this in mind, on top of a thorough evalution of Rabbitat's website, I proposed education as the prospective design focus, and the direction of improving the education experience for prospective and current rabbit adopters navigating the current website. As my evaluation had concluded that Rabbitat’s website is far behind in this area compare to other aspects such as donations and adoptions.

Identified Issues With Current Design

Upon extensive research on the organization, the context, and the website itself, I’ve proposed two main problems of the current website in which we can effectively and realistically resolve given the various constraints.

01

Education not given prominence and is given a lower hierarchy

Looking more into the wild rabbit epidemic in the greater Metro Vancouver area, one main source for this can be traced back to pet abandonment caused by a lack of knowledge when it comes to rabbit keeping. This, on top of Rabbitats goals, means that education should be a key aspect of the webpage.

Yet despite education having its own page, this entire aspect of the page is barely showcased and promoted on the home page, in which heavily reduces engagement. Instead, the focus is placed on the other 3 areas, recruiting volunteers, donations, and adoptions.

02

User-unfriendly education page

On Rabbitat’s education page, the educational content comes in a pdf file format, requiring users to exit the website and read through 50 pages of hard to digest content. The page also includes a singular video from Rabbitat’s YouTube channel and two downloadable pdf files.

The form in which the content comes in is insufficient and not user friendly, and does not promote ease of access for prospective readers. The information being external to the website harms the site’s overall cohesiveness and increases the barrier for information retrieval. This design has also make it extremely difficult for prospective users to access or search for specific content.

Design Iterations

Throughout the iterative process, I pioneered and guided the group’s design direction through my research and leadership skills. My input through iterations was grounded from my research and understanding of Rabbitats, its target audience, and its context. I also facilitated group discussions and encouraged collaboration.

Finalized Improvements

01

Reinventing the hierarchy

Modifying certain elements of the webpage and reinventing the hierarchy to make the education section stand out more for users, encouraging further exploration.

02

Reorganized education material

The educational content has also been organized into well laided out segments for users to navigate through. This design allows the user to find the precise information they are looking for.

Key Reflections

This month long project is one of my first cracks into a time based collaborative design project, as well as the experience of playing more of a leadership role.

One key aspect of this redesign is the need for a realistic yet still effective scoping for the prototype. Given the time limit, I’ve determined that it is key to target specific weak points on the website (after researching the client and its goals) instead of redesigning the entire website. As too large of a scope will result in the team being too spread out, leading to a less effective redesign.

Another key is the importance of proper research, and the need to personalize the design strategy for each client. The main problems and associated solutions are all rooted back to our extensive research on the organization itself and the environmental concerns surrounding it. Without that knowledge and the organization’s needs in mind, the solution would not had been as appropriate.
